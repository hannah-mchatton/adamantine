Energy Planes

The Plane of Positive Energy and Plane of Negative Energy overlay all of the Inner Planes. The Positive Plane is a place of overwhelming light and growth, and the Negative Plane is a plane of death and undeath. Mortals attempting to travel to either are usually slain by their sheer energy.

Denizens of the Positive Plane

Beings of positive energy seem to be composed of pure light. The most well-known of these is the daywalker, which is no less deadly than its negative counterpart. Daywalkers have an overwhelming life aura that causes cancerous tumors to form on their victims.

Denizens of the Negative Plane

Beings of negative energy seem to be composed of pure shadow, and include creatures such as nightwalkers, nightwings, and nightharts. Their mere presence on the Material plane causes life to sap away.

Elemental Planes

The elemental planes exist in four aspects: the Prime Elemental Planes, the Para-Elemental Planes (where two elemental planes overlap with one another), the Quasi-Elemental planes (where an elemental plane overlaps with an energy plane), and the Elemental Chaos (where all sixteen elemental planes overlap).

Prime Elemental Planes

The four prime elemental planes are the largest elemental planes, and cover the most basic elements.

Plane of Air: An endless sky in which floating islands house the few inhabitants. Most flying races, such as aarakocra, trace their roots back to this plane.

Plane of Earth: An expanse of solid earth and stone, rich in ore that draws greedy prospectors.

Plane of Fire: A flaming landscape, where the only survivable location is the City of Brass, ruled by tyrannical efreeti.

Plane of Water: A great sea with islands to explore. Many great beasts of the sea, including krakens, leviathans, and aboleths make their homes here.

Para-Elemental Planes

The four para-elemental planes exist where the prime elemental planes overlap with one another, creating secondary planes.

Plane of Ice (Air/Water): An enormous glacier covered in crevasses that grow smaller and less frequent towards the plane's center.

Plane of Magma (Earth/Fire): A range of volcanoes, bubbling molten rock, and deadly toxic fumes.

Plane of Ooze (Earth/Water): A vast swamp populated by hags and trees infused with dark magic.

Plane of Smoke (Air/Fire): An endless firestorm of smoke and ash, blown by elemental wind.

Quasi-Elemental Planes

The eight quasi-elemental planes exist where the prime elemental planes overlap with one of the energy planes, creating tertiary planes that share their energy plane's deadliness. Few beings live here, and those few are often hardy elementals.

Plane of Ash (Fire/Negative): A place lacking warmth, which saps the heat of anything inside of it.

Plane of Dust (Earth/Negative): A desert coated in constant dust storms so violent they strip creatures down to their bones, and then even grind the bones to dust.

Plane of Lightning (Air/Positive): A vast storm stinking of ozone and inhabited by thunderbirds. The Plane of Lightning occasionally manifests on the Material plane, where it is known as the Perennial Storm.

Plane of Minerals (Earth/Positive): A labyrinthine network of caves rich in gemstones and ore. The gemstones found here are magical, and transform anyone who touches them into gemstones themselves.

Plane of Mist (Water/Positive): A glistening landscape coated in a thin layer of cool mist.

Plane of Radiance (Fire/Positive): An overwhelming bright plane that can easily blind anyone who stares for too long.

Plane of Salt (Water/Negative): An impossibly dry desert landscape, with cracked ground and nary a drop of moisture.

Plane of Vacuum (Air/Negative): A plane of utter emptiness, devoid of light, air, or any true life.

Elemental Chaos

The Elemental Chaos is a roiling, primordial mass of elemental energy surrounding the Inner Planes. Nothing survives in the Chaos for very long, and its unstable nature makes scrying nearly impossible, so very little is known about its depths.

Upper Planes

The Upper Planes are generally planes of light and goodness, the homes of most celestials and other heavenly creatures.

Arcadia

The Peaceable Kingdoms of Arcadia is the plane of LLG alignments. Arcadia is extremely orderly and peaceful, and it is mostly inhabited by peaceful, celestial animals. Hollyphants, magical winged elephants used as war beasts by angels, are native to Arcadia.

Celestia

The Seven Heavens of Mount Celestia is the plane of LG alignments. It is a massive mountain with seven layers, named Lunia, Mercuria, Venya, Solania, Mertion, Jovar, and Chronias. Many gods of good make their homes in Celestia, and the overdeity Chroneria once lived at its very peak. Most species of angels hail from Celestia as well.

Bytopia

The Twin Paradises of Bytopia is the plane of NLG alignments. Bytopia exists in two layers, Dothion and Shurrock. These two layers face each other, either side of a barrier such that one forms in the sky of the other. Dothion is largely a layer of meadows and forests, while Shurrock mostly contains mountainous, hilly terrain. Both are subject to extreme seasons and weather changes.

Elysium

The Blessed Fields of Elysium is the plane of NG alignments. Elysium is situated around the great River Oceanus, the holy counterpart to the River Styx found in the Lower Planes.

Beastlands

The Wilderness of the Beastlands is the plane of NCG alignments. The Beastlands consist of endless savannahs, marshes, grasslands, forests, and other natural terrain inhabited by sentient animals. Each of its three layers are locked in a specific time, Krigala at noon, Brux at dusk, and Karasuthra at midnight. Creatures who spend sufficient time in the Beastlands often find themselves slowly changing into one of the plane's sentient animals.

Arborea

The Olympian Glades of Arborea is the plane of CG alignments. It is also called Arvandor by elves, or Olympus by those who pay homage to the Greek pantheon. The plane is a land of emotions, where every feeling is amplified. Best described as boisterous, it is a vibrant, passionate place with strong emotions of both good and evil.

Ysgard

The Heroic Domains of Ysgard is the plane of CCG alignments. The Norse pantheon comes from Ysgard, as do the souls of great warriors and heroes. Ysgard is an endless battlefield, its terrain composed of gorges, mountains, and rivers. Giants originated in Ysgard, where they are still found to this day. Ysgardian giants are typically larger and more intelligent than their brethren however.

Lower Planes

The Lower Planes are generally planes of darkness and evil, the homes of most fiends and other hellish creatures.

Acheron

The Infinite Battlefield of Acheron is the plane of LLE alignments. The plane consists of four enormous iron cubes the size of nations, on which constant battles rage. The cubes occasionally touch, allowing creatures on them to travel between cubes if they wish. Like Ysgard above, Acheron's inhabitants include the souls of warriors.

Baator

The Nine Hells of Baator is the plane of LE alignments. Devils hail from the Nine Hells, and wage an endless war against the demons known as the Blood War. The River Styx's mouth is located in the Nine Hells, in its highest layer of Avernus.

Avernus

Avernus, the first of the Nine Hells, is the battlefield of the Blood War. The demons from the Abyss spill out of the River Styx on to the fields of Avernus, where they are cut down by devils. Avernus was once ruled by the fallen angel Zariel, but after her redemption it passed back to Bel, its original ruler. All kinds of devils are found here in the battle, but abashai, lemures, nupperibos, and spinagons are native.

Dis

Dis, the second of the Nine Hells, is a massive city of devils, crowded and oppressive no matter where one goes within it. Dispater rules Dis from his Iron Tower, and is known for his caution and foresight. Imps are native to Dis, but all varities of devil can be found here.

Minauros

Minauros, the third of the Nine Hells, is a vast, putrid swamp. The ruler of Minauros is Mammon, archdevil of greed. Soul coins are created here, and Mammon's greed prevents any real improvement from happening in the swamps. Barbazus, hamatulas, excruciarchs, and kytons are native to Minauros.

Phlegethos

Phlegethos, the fourth of the Nine Hells, is a desolate, fiery place. The rulers of Phlegethos are Fierna and Belial, a rare partnership among devils. Fierna is passionate and volatile, while Belial is cold and calculating. Both are very fond of political schemes and both have a way with words. Hellhounds are the most notable devils native to Phlegethos.

Stygia

Stygia, the fifth of the Nine Hells, is a plane of ice. A vast, cold sea filled with icebergs and floes. Levistus is the ruler, perpetually frozen by Asmodeus for unknown reasons. Gelugons are native to Stygia, but little else is.

Malbolge

Malbolge, the sixth of the Nine Hells, is an endless slope down which boulders constantly roll. Malbolge's ruler is Glasya, the daughter of Asmodeus. She is extremely unpredictable, and has a penchant for manipulation. She is also known to be Baator's most wanted criminal. Osyluths, paeliryons, merregons, erinyes, succubi, and incubi are native to Malbolge.

Maladomini

Maladomini, the seventh of the Nine Hells, is a labyrinth of information and bureaucracy. Its ruler, Baalzebul, is known for being exceedinly cautious, planning out each and every one of his actions. Amnizus are native here.

Cania

Cania, the eighth of the Nine Hells, is a plane of rapidly moving glaciers. The cold here seems to have a life of its own, consciously sucking heat out of every living being. Mephistopheles rules Cania, a devilish scientist with a fondness for arcane magic. Gelugons are also native to Cania.

Nessus

Nessus, the ninth of the Nine Hells, is a featureless plain containing the city of Malsheem, from which Asmodeus rules the Nine Hells. The most powerful of devils are native here, including pit fiends.

Carceri

The Tarterian Depths of Carceri is the plane of NLE alignments. A vast prison inhabited by the demodands, Carceri consists of concentric, spherical layers.

Hades

The Grey Wastes of Hades is the plane of NE alignments. A plane of apathy, devoid of emotion, Hades is a dark and gloomy place. Yugoloths can be found here, among other fiends.

Gehenna

The Bleak Eternity of Gehenna is the plane of NCE alignments. Gehenna consists of four supervolcanoes that constantly belch acrid fumes and lava, making the plane inhospitable for almost any creature. Yugoloths are native to Gehenna, having been created here by a coven of night hags. The only known civilization found here is the Crawling City, a moving city ruled by the General of Gehenna. The General is the patron deity of yugoloths, and is never found by anyone who he doesn't want to see.

Abyss

The Abyss is the plane of CE alignments. A jumbled mess of hundreds of layers, it was once thought to be infinite. Demons hail from the Abyss, and the only way out is through the River Styx, causing the Blood War by stumbling across Avernus in the process. The Abyss was created long ago by Tharizdun, the overdeity of entropy, and he is now imprisoned within its depths.

1: Pazunia

Pazunia is a barren wasteland of scorching winds under a relentless red sun. Its surface is covered by portals leading deeper into the Abyss. Pazunia is named for its ruler, Pazuzu.

45-47: Azzagrat

Azzagrat is the realm of Grazz't, demon prince of pleasure. Grazz't was once a devil, but defected to the side of the demons.

66: Demonweb Pits

The Demonweb Pits is a network of spider webs that fold on to each other. Lolth rules this layer, both in her capacity as a goddess and in her capacity as a demon lord.

88: Gaping Maw

This layer consists of a vast continent covered in jungle surrounded by an endless ocean and brine flats. It is the realm of Demogorgon, the Prince of Demons, one of the oldest and highest-ranking demons in the Abyss.

113: Thanatos

This layer is a land of ruin and death, with sterile heaths, black forests, and desolate mountains filled with ruins and scattered tombs, inhabited by a variety of undead. It is ruled by Orcus, demon prince of undeath, who was the first to uncover the secrets of lichdom.

176: Hollow's Heart

This layer contains the realm of the demon lord Fraz-Urb'luu, the Prince of Deception and demon lord of illusions. Hollow's Heart is a continent-sized landmass surrounded by fine white powder, all under a starless black sky. Despite this, the ground is lit as brightly as if by a noon sun.

222: Shedaklah

The layer is a bubbling fetid swamp, covered with oozes, molds and slimes that formed strange organic shapes, under a mud-brown sky overcast with green clouds from which torrential rains of filth fell every few hours. Both Zuggtmoy and Juiblex lay claim to this layer, and continually fight for control.

422: Death Dells

The Death Dells consist of an endless hunting ground of barren hills and ravines, with a few structures and scattered signs of civilization. Yeenoghu, the beast of butchery and creator of gnolls, rules this plane.

600: Endless Maze

The Endless Maze is an infinite, labyrinthine layer ruled by Baphomet. The demon lord of minotaurs stalks through his maze to frighten all of the layer's inhabitants.

Blackhole

Blackhole is located at the very bottom of the Abyss. It consists of nothing more than a small, rocky island floating in a sea of black nothingness. Tharizdun is held in Blackhole. Even though the chained god possesses all of his power and has no literal chains binding him, he is unable to leave due to the wards placed on his layer. So he simply waits, growing ever more insane with every minute that passes.

Pandemonium

The Windswept Depths of Pandemonium is the plane of CCE alignments. Pandemonium consists of an endless cave network, through which howling winds constantly blow. These winds are loud and strong enough to drive even the most stalwart mind to madness, and so very little chooses to live here.

Other Outer Planes

Outlands

The Outlands is a disk-shaped plane, usually seen as the plane of N alignments. Located around its edge are the gate-towns, small cities that have some of the only permanent portals to their respective outer planes. At the center of the Outlands is the Spire, a jutting spike of rock that holds aloft Sigil, the City of Doors. Sigil is ruled by the Lady of Pain, and it is said that anywhere in the multiverse can be accessed from Sigil if you know how.

Jazirah

Jazirah is a strange plane connected to the Outlands. Consisting of an endless desert under a gray sun, it was once colonized by the sarrukh, among other ancient races. Little is known about it, as most attempts to explore the plane have ended in failure. What is known is that the great pyramid at its center imprisons Dendar, the overdeity of darkness.

Mechanus

The Clockwork Nirvana of Mechanus is the plane of LN alignments. Mechanus is home to the modrons, biomechanical beings created by the overdeity Primus. The purpose of the modrons is unknown, but they march to catalogue the multiverse once every 289 years. Other constructs, including the inevitables, can also be found here.

Limbo

The Ever-Changing Chaos of Limbo is the plane of CN alignments. Limbo is a primordial soup of magic and chaos, able to be harnessed and shaped by anyone with a strong enough mind. The natives of Limbo are the slaadi, frog-like creatures that are infamous for their regenerative abilities.

Realms Beyond

The Astral Realm is but one realm of the vast multiverse. Each realm has its own cosmology and laws of magic, entirely alien from the others.

Uvuu

Uvuu is the most well-known of the alternate realms. Most people within the Astral Realm know it as simply the Far Realm, but its true name is Uvuu. Many of the aberrative threats found within the Astral Realm, including aboleths, beholders, and illithids, are tied to the realm of Uvuu, most simply being visitors from that realm.

Uvuu is ruled by the Uvuudaum, from which the realm draws its name. These strange, malevolent creatures are so alien that even existing within the Astral Realm seems to bend space around them, and instill madness in others.
