Energy Planes

The Plane of Positive Energy and Plane of Negative Energy overlay all of the Inner Planes. The Positive Plane is a place of overwhelming light and growth, and the Negative Plane is a plane of death and undeath. Mortals attempting to travel to either are usually slain by their sheer energy.

Denizens of the Positive Plane

Beings of positive energy seem to be composed of pure light. The most well-known of these is the daywalker, which is no less deadly than its negative counterpart. Daywalkers have an overwhelming life aura that causes cancerous tumors to form on their victims.

Denizens of the Negative Plane

Beings of negative energy seem to be composed of pure shadow, and include creatures such as nightwalkers, nightwings, and nightharts. Their mere presence on the Material plane causes life to sap away.

Elemental Planes

The elemental planes exist in four aspects: the Prime Elemental Planes, the Para-Elemental Planes (where two elemental planes overlap with one another), the Quasi-Elemental planes (where an elemental plane overlaps with an energy plane), and the Elemental Chaos (where all sixteen elemental planes overlap).

Prime Elemental Planes

The four prime elemental planes are the largest elemental planes, and cover the most basic elements.

Plane of Air: An endless sky in which floating islands house the few inhabitants. Most flying races, such as aarakocra, trace their roots back to this plane. Plane of Earth: An expanse of solid earth and stone, rich in ore that draws greedy prospectors. Plane of Fire: A flaming landscape, where the only survivable location is the City of Brass, ruled by tyrannical efreeti. Plane of Water: A great sea with islands to explore. Many great beasts of the sea, including krakens, leviathans, and aboleths make their homes here.
Para-Elemental Planes

The four para-elemental planes exist where the prime elemental planes overlap with one another, creating secondary planes.

Plane of Ice (Air/Water): An enormous glacier covered in crevasses that grow smaller and less frequent towards the plane's center. Plane of Magma (Earth/Fire): A range of volcanoes, bubbling molten rock, and deadly toxic fumes. Plane of Ooze (Earth/Water): A vast swamp populated by hags and trees infused with dark magic. Plane of Smoke (Air/Fire): An endless firestorm of smoke and ash, blown by elemental wind.
Quasi-Elemental Planes

The eight quasi-elemental planes exist where the prime elemental planes overlap with one of the energy planes, creating tertiary planes that share their energy plane's deadliness. Few beings live here, and those few are often hardy elementals.

Plane of Ash (Fire/Negative): A place lacking warmth, which saps the heat of anything inside of it. Plane of Dust (Earth/Negative): A desert coated in constant dust storms so violent they strip creatures down to their bones, and then even grind the bones to dust. Plane of Lightning (Air/Positive): A vast storm stinking of ozone and inhabited by thunderbirds. The Plane of Lightning occasionally manifests on the Material plane, where it is known as the Perennial Storm. Plane of Minerals (Earth/Positive): A labyrinthine network of caves rich in gemstones and ore. The gemstones found here are magical, and transform anyone who touches them into gemstones themselves. Plane of Mist (Water/Positive): A glistening landscape coated in a thin layer of cool mist. Plane of Radiance (Fire/Positive): An overwhelming bright plane that can easily blind anyone who stares for too long. Plane of Salt (Water/Negative): An impossibly dry desert landscape, with cracked ground and nary a drop of moisture. Plane of Vacuum (Air/Negative): A plane of utter emptiness, devoid of light, air, or any true life.
