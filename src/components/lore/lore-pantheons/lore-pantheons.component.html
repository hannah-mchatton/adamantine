The Small Council

Among the overdeities, there is a group known as the Small Council. Few know of its existence, and no one knows its purpose. The Small Council consists of Amaunator, Ao, Chroneria, Dendar, Mystryl, Pandorym, Praeteria, Primus, and Quilrith.

The Writer

The mysterious being known as the Writer is an overdeity that very few can even contact, let alone wield the power of. The Writer is thought to be a mostly prophetic being, someone whose writings match up with the events that transpire in the multiverse. Whatever power the Writer actually has to guide these events is unknown.

Amaunator

Amaunator, known as Lathander in some spheres, is the primordial god of light and birth. He commonly appears as a paladin in shining armor and wielding a heavy mace, and he is the most likely to interact with mortals.

As a paladin, Amaunator is best described as kind, caring, and loving. Always eager to lend a hand to those in need, Ao often has to reign him in and prevent him from interacting too much in the lives of mortals.

Ao

Ao is thought to be the first of the overdeities, second only to The Writer. He often appears as an old man in deep blue robes, carrying a pair of stone tablets. Ao policies the actions of the other overdeities, preventing them from interacting with mortals too often.

Ao is a mysterious deity, rarely seen only in times of dire need. His last appearance was at the end of the Spellplague, where he managed to right many of its damages. Outside of his intentional appearances, Ao is not known to ever interact with mortals in any fashion.

Chroneria

Chroneria was once the overdeity of time, a beautiful woman with sky blue hair. Not long ago, she attempted to wield the Aethergem to take control of the entire multiverse, but she was stopped by Quilrith and the adventurers who she tried to get to do her bidding.

Chroneria was once a much more neutral deity. She simply existed, ensuring that others didn't cause harm to the stream of time. Why exactly she became power-hungry is unknown.

Dendar

Dendar is the primordial goddess of night. Almost always in the form of an enormous black cobra, she was imprisoned long ago in a strange plane known as Jazirah. Dendar constantly plots her escape, and almost succeeded not long ago.

When Dendar was free, she was a slithery, manipulative being who brought many people under her sway. Those following Dendar were often promised their wildest dreams, promises that Dendar had no intentions of honoring.

Lady of Pain

The enigmatic being known as the Lady of Pain is a deity of neutrality, one with the power to rewrite the multiverse, though few know of her true godly nature. The Lady rules Sigil, the city of doors, and violently eschews all worship. She is rarely seen and only ever acts in times of extreme emergency. At all other times, she simply keeps watch over her city and holds the multiverse in check.

Mystryl

Mystryl, the ancient goddess of magic, was the very architect of the Spellweave that all other magic in the multiverse calls upon. In some ways, Mystryl is the Spellweave herself. The wife of Primus, she long ago entered Limbo to investigate a strange anomaly, and never returned.

Mystryl often creates lesser versions of herself, often named Mystra, to police the magic users of most spheres. Though Mystryl gains the memories of these lesser deities, they are independent beings with their own thoughts, emotions, and goals.

[null]

Little is known about the god of chaos [null]. It is known that this god is the counterpart to Primus' law, but the god itself has not been seen nor heard of as far as anyone can remember. It is thought that [null] is in some way connected to Limbo, plane of chaos, but investigations have all turned up dry.

Pandorym

Pandorym was once a nearly unstoppable force of the Astral Void, a sort of living, evil sphere of annihilation the size of a small asteroid. The only way it could be mitigated was to separate its essence from its body, trapping the essence within a crystal sphere. Which crystal sphere and where within it has been lost to history.

Praeteria

Praeteria was the creator of the original humanoid races. Few of her original creations remain, but her legacy can be seen in all life. She rarely appears, usually prefering to exist as a sort of live-giving mana that permeates the multiverse, but when she does appear it is as a dryad.

Primus

Primus is the god of order and law, and the creator of the modron race. In his Hall of Concordance on the plane of Mechanus, he serves to keep the multiverse stable. The modron race is effectively an extension of himself, and it is countless in number.

Quilrith

Quilrith is usually regarded as the leader of the Small Council by those few who know his true nature. He normally takes the form of a dragonborn and travels the multiverse. However, if any threats to the multiverse present themselves, he takes upon the form of a great beast to restore balance.

Quilrith is known for occasionally bringing together groups of adventurers to defend the multiverse. These groups often pull beings from across spheres that never interact with one another, even bringing together the most magical and most technological beings.

Tharizdun

Tharizdun is mostly known as the creator of the Abyss. Corrupted by influence from Uvuu, he was given a seed of chaos and told to plant it within the Astral Sea. Knowing the other gods would catch on to this and stop him, he instead planted it in the Elemental Chaos, where it grew and festered into the Abyss.

When his creation grew out of control, the other gods elected to imprison him deep within it. The lowest layer of the Abyss, Blackhole, is Tharizdun's eternal prison. Despite his imprisonment, he wields all of his original power, he merely lacks the means to escape. His time spent in Blackhole has slowly driven him even more insane.

Asgorath

Asgorath, or Io, was the dragon god of creation. Having created all of dragonkind long ago, he is sometimes equated with concepts of the World Serpent, and is sometimes even considered a primordial. Asgorath disappeared before the time of man, leaving no trace as to his whereabouts.

Ashardalon

Ashardalon is the god of solar dragons, with a star for a heart and a love of exploring. He is thought to have been the one who first shared the secrets of the Astral Sea with mortals, by bringing the skeletons of kindori to the world of Thenix.

Ashardalon always manifests as a solar dragon, beaming intense radiance that makes it difficult to look directly at him.

Bahamut

Brother to Null and Tiamat, Bahamut is the paladin-like god of metallic dragons. He makes his home on Mount Celestia, and works to fight against the machinations of his sister.

Bahamut's draconic form is an immense dragon with platinum scales, a color rarely seen in mortal metallic dragons. In human form, he takes the shape of an old man, often accompanied by seven golden canaries. These canaries are actually seven gold great wyrms, members of his royal guard. The current members of his guard are named Gruemar, Kurya, Marroshok, Sonngrad, Troannaxia, Urgala, and Vulcan.

Garyx

Garyx is a violent, hateful, cruel red dragon who ascended to godhood as the draconic god of destruction. Sometimes even considered a demon lord for his incredible violence and chaos, he occasionally appears simply to cause mayhem. He is most well known for his rivalry with the giant god Annam, a conflict that eventually resulted in the destruction of the giant kingdom Ostoria.

Kereska

Kereska is the draconic goddess of magic. Sometimes considered an aspect of Mystryl, she specifically governs over draconic magic. Kereska is creative, and is thought to have been the dragon who figured out how to manifest latent magic as breath weapons.

Kereska is quite fond of shapeshifting, and as such has no determined form. However, she is always in the form of a dragon, and never takes human shape.

Lendys

Father of Bahamut, Null, and Tiamat, Lendys is the draconic god of law and justice. More strict and lawful than his son Bahamut, his judgement is absolute and he has little sense of mercy.

Lendys' draconic form is a wingless platinum dragon. His eyes blaze with light, and it is said that it is impossible to fool Lendys.

Nathair

Nathair is the mischievious, pie-loving god of faerie dragons and pseudodragons. A member of the Summer Court, he rarely spends time with the other archfey, prefering instead to engage in various shenanigans with many kinds of lesser fey. Titania and Oberon often consider him their court jester, and sometimes grow weary of his silliness.

Nathair's draconic form is that of a faerie dragon. He is the smallest of the dragon gods, only a couple of feet long, and has an eternal grin on his face.

Null

Sibling to Bahamut and Tiamat, Null is the first dracolich, as well as the first shadow dragon. They met Orcus long ago and learned the secret to lichdom from the demon lord, then adapting the highly complex magic to function on draconic bodies. Null is a pessimist who truly hates their siblings and wants nothing to do with either of them.

Null's draconic form takes the shape of a region of blackness in the shape of a dragon. Their voice is cold and slithery, and it is said that to touch Null is to instantly die. They never take human form.

Raulothim

Raulothim is the paranoid, contemplative god of emerald dragons. He is solitary and actively avoids contact with others, but he enjoys learning information from afar. He rarely leaves his lair, contemplating the nature of the multiverse and the dangers posed by multiversal travelers.

Rime

Rime, goddess of white dragons, is a tragic figure. Before her ascension to godhood, she was forced to serve as mount for an ancient order, and had mithral plates magically fused to her body. Though the order of knights who subjugated her have long since died out, the physical and mental scars the treatment left on her have resulted in her being ever suspicious of those around her, especially humanoids. Still bound by her shackles, she seeks freedom from the magic that binds them to her.

Sardior

Sardior is the psionic god of gem dragons. Playful and conversational, he has an immensely sharp wit and often uses his magic to bestow psionics on others.

Sardior's draconic form is a gem dragon with ruby-colored scales, an extremely rare species of dragon. He is often mistaken for a red dragon due to his similar appearance.

Tamara

Mother of Bahamut, Null, and Tiamat, Tamara is the goddess of life, mercy, and compassion. Serving to balance her husband Lendys, Bahamut is often considered the best aspects of each of them. She is among the most benevolent deities.

Tamara's draconic form is a wingless platinum dragon smaller than her husband or son. She is said to radiate warmth and love.

Task

Task is the greed of a dragon personified. He wants to own everything, and won't be satisfied until he has it all.

Task's draconic form is a relatively small dragon. His exact color is difficult to discern because of the vast amount of wealth he wears, but he is likely some form of gem dragon.

Tiamat

Sister to Bahamut and Null, Tiamat is evil, manipulative the goddess of chromatic dragons. Banished to Averus, first of the Nine Hells, she constantly plots returning to the material plane to enact her revenge on Bahamut.

Tiamat's draconic form is a five-headed dragon, with each head belonging to a different species of chromatic dragon. She also possesses the stinger of a wyvern on the tip of her tail, though her venom is far more deadly. In human form, she takes the shape of a tall, slender woman with yellowish skin and dark hair.
