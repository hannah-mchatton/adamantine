Gaining a Transformation

Transformations are usually gained unwillingly through a variety of methods, which vary between transformations. All transformations have two kinds of requirements: an ability score requirement and a role play requirement.

The ability score requirement must be met in order to access any of the transformation's features. The transformation can still technically be attained, but none of its boons or flaws will be conferred unless the ability score requiement is met.

The role play requirement is more vague, usually requiring a different powerful being to grant you the transformation, such as being bit by a vampire or contracting lycanthropy.

The Lich transformation has the additional requirement of being able to cast 7th-level spells.

Leveling Up a Transformation

Transformations have four levels, each of which is intended to correspond with a tier of play. In order to level up a transformation from one level to the next, you must complete one of its milestones, listed with each transformation. When a transformation gains a level, you gain a new flaw and a new boon of your choice.

If you accidentally complete a milestone and wish to resist your transformation growing in strength, the DM may call for a Charisma saving throw to not give in.

If a transformation is at 4th level, you gain an additional transformation boon for every two milestones you gain.

Ending a Transformation

Transformations of 3rd-level or lower can be ended through use of the Remove Curse spell. When cast in this way, the spellcaster must succeed on an ability check with their spellcasting ability, with a DC equal to 13 + double the level of the transformation. On a failed check, the spell slot is wasted and the transformation does not end.

If a transformation progresses to 4th-level, it can't be ended by any means short of the Wish spell.
