The Aethergem

The Aethergem is a gemstone of pure astral energy. Forged from artifacts from across the multiverse, it possesses unparalleled power. The Aethergem is so powerful in fact that it can strip the divine powers of any god, even an overdeity, changing them into a mere mortal. It was once split apart, but was recently reforged by the adventurers who Chroneria manipulated into doing her bidding.

The Clown

Multiversal travel is a difficult thing to do without the Astral Sea, but at least one being has managed to do it. A strange, clown-like being travels the multiverse through unnatural means. Her portals resemble less planar portals and more holes in the multiverse itself.

Below is a non-exhaustive list of known spheres of the Astral Sea.

Items marked with ε are part of Sector ε, the area near Primespace that contains the most densely clustered crystal spheres.

Items marked with μ are additional Astral Manifestations.

Primespaceε

This massive sphere in the center of the Astral Sea contains an enormous, possibly limitless, universe. Earth, or Terra, is the only planet within known to harbor any life, but the universe within Primespace is so massive that no spelljammer has fully mapped it. Primespace is notably very resistant to magic. While spelljammers can still fly within its walls and magic required for creatures to exist (such as the animating magic of constructs) still functions, all other magic simply fails.

Atomspaceε

This sphere contains an Earth ravaged by nuclear war. The few survivors eke out a modest existence, but this world is far from the technological splendor it once wielded.

Arthurspaceε

This sphere contains an Earth inhabited by the heroes of old. The most prominent of these heroes includes Arthur, king of England. Uniquely, this sphere contains a geocentric world, as opposed to the typical heliocentric model.

Barberaspaceε

This sphere contains an Earth on which a group of misfits and their dog spend time solving minor mysteries. Most of these mysteries turn out to be nothing more than misdirection, but a few are genuinely supernatural.

Berspace

This sphere contains a world called Eberron. Though primarily a plane of magic, Eberron contains numerous technological wonders. The nations of Eberron have recently come out of a massive world war that resulted in the deaths of countless people.

Burntspace

This sphere contains a world called Athas, a dying desert world that even the gods have forsaken. The rulers of Athas are infamous for kidnapping people and creatures from other planes to use in their gladiatorial games. The magic used here draws directly on the life-force of creatures around the spellcaster, and even on the energies of the planet itself, a danger that caused the downfall of Athas' ecosystem.

Doomspace

This sphere was destroyed long ago by unknown means. Now, it exists as an astral system surrounded by the fragments of its shell. Doomspace consists of three planets and the nine moons of a gas giant which no longer exists in slowly decaying orbits around a massive black hole called the Eye of Doom. Those that still live in Doomspace are a hardy folk, and it is a dangerous place to venture for anyone. Criminal enterprises abound, and one planet hosts violent games between bound gladiators.

Folkspaceε

This sphere contains an Earth on which all folk heroes from Earth exist, and do the things that their stories say they do, resulting in a place with very little war or violence. The heroes of Folkspace rarely leave their sphere, as they lose much of their special abilities outside of it.

Forcespaceε

This sphere near Primespace contains a highly technologically advanced universe where a totalitarian government called the Galactic Empire holds sway. Spelljammers mostly avoid this sphere, as the Empire's ships possess incredible firepower and they are always vigilant.

Among the unique threats of Forcespace, there also exists a species similar to the gith, known as the Yuuzhan Vong. These zealotrous warriors were cut off from the natural magic of their sphere, and a few reckless members of their society tried to regain access, becoming the first illithids in the process. The gith were the results of these mad Vong enslaving and experimenting on their own peoples.

Hyperspaceμ

Hyperspace is an Astral Manifestation bound to Forcespace that allows for FTL travel. Hyperspace also has numerous variants, most notably Otherspace.

Furyspaceε

This sphere contains an Earth badly ravaged by war over resources. The only continent that still holds any life is Australia, and what life it does hold is hard. Gasoline is seen as a vital resource that is even more important than water.

Goldenspace

This odd sphere on the far reaches of the Crystal Fields contains a highly magical universe called Hyrule. It is among the few crystal spheres with a particularly distinct Feywild and Shadowfell, each of which contain very different locations than the space itself.

Goldenspace's Feywild

Goldenspace's Feywild, known within it as the Sacred Realm, is a realm of light and life. The gods of Goldenspace live here, watching over and protecting the plane. A temple within Goldenspace is the only known way to access the Sacred Realm.

Goldenspace's Shadowfell

Goldenspace's Shadowfell, known within it as the Dark Realm or Lorule, is a dark mirror of its material. It also has numerous Domains of Dread, each with different rules and magic. These include Termina, a plane of loss, and the Twilight Realm, a plane of betrayal.

Greyspace

This sphere contains a world called Oerth, from which many famous wizards and sorcerers hail. The most well known of these is Mordenkainen, a notable wizard who travels the planes in his research.

Guildspace

This sphere contains a plane-wide city called Ravnica, which governed by seven powerful guilds. The guilds each have different views on how the city should be run, and each are established in a different niche. However, despite their differences, the guilds usually manage to maintain a relative sense of peace.

Hammerspaceε

This sphere contains a technologically advanced galaxy that is always in a state of constant and brutal warfare. Powerful demon lords were trapped here long ago, and as such it is difficult to enter or leave Hammerspace.

Hammerspace's Feywild

The Feywild of Hammerspace is different from its material in only one respect: it uses magic where its material would use technology. It is no less dangerous or violent than its material, and may even be more so depending on your perspective.

The Warpμ

The Warp is an Astral Manifestation similar to the Astral Sea bound to Hammerspace that is used as a FTL travel system by its inhabitants. It is also the home of most of the demons found in Hammerspace, and as such is very dangerous to cross.

Hoxxespace

This sphere contains a cluster of worlds known as the Hoxxes cluster, on which teams of dwarves mine for valuable resources and encounter alien lifeforms.

Ioraspace

This sphere contains a world called Iora, a world of shallow oceans and archipelagos. The Ioran peoples, mainly praedarens, were once merely pirate factions. Eventually, one of these factions managed to unite their world under one banner. Eventually, the Ioran Ascendancy, as it came to be known, took control over other nearby crystal spheres.

Krynnspace

This sphere contains a world called Krynn, a world of magic and dark sorcery. The gods of this sphere see the planet of Krynn as a chessboard, and perform endless games across its surface.

Moonspace

This sphere contains many worlds existing in tandem, collectivelly called Terraria. A variety of beings exist on these worlds, most of them aberrative in nature, including Cthulu's younger brother, known here as the Moon Lord.

Moonspace is known to have a particular infection from the Abyss known as the Crimson, a living hive mind that desires to consume the entire sphere.

Mythspace

This sphere contains a world called Theros, full of adventurers and monsters. The sphere's name comes from the fact that, more than most, this sphere's inhabitants bear a striking resemblance to the myths of Earth.

Nuispace

This sphere contains numerous bio-mechanical beings, living within and fighting for control of the body of a dead god. This plane-wide war has been raging for centuries, and every being within the sphere contributes to it in some way or another. Many of the bio-mechanical beings in the sphere possess elemental pwoers, and most of them bear magical masks.

Quspaceε

This sphere contains the Local Group of galaxies, which in this universe have been ravaged by eons of war and genetic manipulation. The humans who live here are completely unrecognizable as humans due to their long and tragic evolutionary history.

Pokéspace

This sphere contains a world on which intelligent animals with elemental powers, called Pokémon, are used by the humans of this world as pets. These creatures enjoy fighting, and their human companions often battle them together.

Popspace

This sphere contains a star-shaped planet called Popstar, which is inhabited by cutesy creatures that are secretly horrifying. A powerful but surprisingly gentle demon known as the Great Devourer makes his home here. The Great Devourer could destroy the entire multiverse if he wanted to, but he generally just likes eating cake.

Popspace's Shadowfell

The Shadowfell of Popspace, known within it as the Mirror Realm, is a dark mirror of its material inhabited by dark versions of its material beings. Among these is a dark version of the Great Devourer, who is more mischevous than anything.

Realmspace

This sphere contains a universe known as the Forgotten Realms, in which a continent called Faerun holds many adventures. The space around Realmspace is highly trafficked due to Faerun's allure both as an adventurer and as a merchant. Spelljammers are surprisingly rare here, and some in Faerun fear them due to run-ins with illithids.

The Hellriders

The Hellriders are a loose batallion of paladins who fight against the forces of Baator. Once upon a time, the Hellriders had lost their way and become just as horrible as the devils they fight. Recently, their commander Zariel was redeemed from her devilish state. She still doesn't find a home with the other angels due to her history and warrior spirit, so she returned to commanding the Hellriders alongside her friend Voldrov, who was the paladin that helped her find herself again. Voldrov and Zariel are very close, and more than friends, but are not lovers.

Scrollspace

This sphere contains a fantastical world called Nirn, where adventurers perform a vast array of different activities. Most of the citizens of Scrollspace are rather stupid, and will fall for the simplest of tricks. The exception to this is the dark gods of this sphere, the Daedra.

Scrollspace's Shadowfell

The Shadowfell of Scrollspace is a dark place of magic and fire known within the sphere as the Realms of Oblivion. The Daedra hail from here.

Shovelspace

This sphere contains a small area called the Valley of Landurr. Varied, unique knights live in this valley, most of whom wield shovels.

Slayerspaceε

This sphere contains a Sol system where Mars is overrun by devilish forces and a warrior known as the Doom Slayer often appears to push them back. Over its history, the Earth of Slayerspace has been taken over by the forces of Hell multiple times, only to be pushed back by the Doom Slayer.

Slayerspace's Feywild

The Feywild of Slayerspace is inhabited by a knightly order commonly known as the Argents. The Doom Slayer is one of these Argents, but he has since become disgraced due to his actions against the forces of Hell.

Slayerspace's Shadowfell

The Shadowfell of Slayerspace was taken over by devilish forces from the Nine Hells long ago, specifically by an archdevil named Davoth. It launches regular assaults against its material that the Doom Slayer exists to fight against.

Sparkspaceε

This sphere contains a planet called Cybertron, which was inhabited by robotic life-forms waging a massive civil war. The war has since spilled across the universe, most notably to Earth. Primus is particularly fond of this sphere, and one of his most powerful avatars is found there.

Sparkspace's Shadowfell

The Shadowfell of Sparkspace is often referred to as the Shattered Glass. It is an alternate dimension where the warring factions of Cybertron are flipped, as are the personalities of their members. A corrupted avatar of Primus is also found here.

Tennospaceε

This sphere is a technologically advanced version of the Sol System called the Origin System. Much of the sphere's long and nuanced history is completely unknown to its inhabitants. The sphere is named for the Tenno, a class of mystical warriors that hail from this sphere, taking control of powerful biomechanical bodies called Warframes to fight.

Thenispace

This sphere in a highly chaotic region of the Astral Sea contains a magical world called Thenix. Thought to be the birthplace of the dragon gods Bahamut and Tiamat, Thenix is rarely visited by spelljammers due to the storms within the surrounding phlogiston.

Turtlespaceε

This sphere contains an Earth on which several anthropormophized animals live, most notably four turtle brothers named after Rennaisance artists. These brothers secretly fight against a clan of ninjas called the Foot.

These four spheres are not known and mapped by spelljammers prior to the events of Echoes of the Astral Sea.

Darkspace

This sphere contains a world of gothic horror called Innistrad. Vampires, werewolves, zombies, and more abound. It was once trapped in a state of perpetual night, but its daylight has since been restored.

Eezospaceε

This sphere contains a Milky Way galaxy in a series of cycles, where an ancient robotic race called Reapers will clear out the inhabitants once every few eons. For reasons unknown, phlogiston can exist within this sphere, where it is called Element Zero.

Halospaceε

This sphere contains a Milky Way galaxy inhabited by humans as well as numerous alien races. For decades, the humans were locked in a struggle for control of the Halo superweapons with the Covenant, a conglomeration of aliens, but the war has recently ended.

Slipspaceμ

Slipspace is an Astral Manifestation bound to Halospace that allows for FTL travel. It exists as a tangle of magical dimensions, above the standard three-dimensional world of Halospace.

Witchspace

This sphere near Sector ε contains a world inhabited by humans, elves, and monsters of many kinds. Witchspace has a unique history, in that it collided with a sphere in Sector ε long ago, resulting in the other sphere's destruction and Witchspace gaining its unique magic. Humans were also brought to Witchspace in this event.
