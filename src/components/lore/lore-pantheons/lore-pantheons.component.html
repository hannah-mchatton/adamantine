The Small Council

Among the overdeities, there is a group known as the Small Council. Few know of its existence, and no one knows its purpose. The Small Council consists of Amaunator, Ao, Chroneria, Dendar, Mystryl, Pandorym, Praeteria, Primus, and Quilrith.

The Writer

The mysterious being known as the Writer is an overdeity that very few can even contact, let alone wield the power of. The Writer is thought to be a mostly prophetic being, someone whose writings match up with the events that transpire in the multiverse. Whatever power the Writer actually has to guide these events is unknown.

Amaunator

Amaunator, known as Lathander in some spheres, is the primordial god of light and birth. He commonly appears as a paladin in shining armor and wielding a heavy mace, and he is the most likely to interact with mortals.

As a paladin, Amaunator is best described as kind, caring, and loving. Always eager to lend a hand to those in need, Ao often has to reign him in and prevent him from interacting too much in the lives of mortals.

Ao

Ao is thought to be the first of the overdeities, second only to The Writer. He often appears as an old man in deep blue robes, carrying a pair of stone tablets. Ao policies the actions of the other overdeities, preventing them from interacting with mortals too often.

Ao is a mysterious deity, rarely seen only in times of dire need. His last appearance was at the end of the Spellplague, where he managed to right many of its damages. Outside of his intentional appearances, Ao is not known to ever interact with mortals in any fashion.

Chroneria

Chroneria was once the overdeity of time, a beautiful woman with sky blue hair. Not long ago, she attempted to wield the Aethergem to take control of the entire multiverse, but she was stopped by Quilrith and the adventurers who she tried to get to do her bidding.

Chroneria was once a much more neutral deity. She simply existed, ensuring that others didn't cause harm to the stream of time. Why exactly she became power-hungry is unknown.

Dendar

Dendar is the primordial goddess of night. Almost always in the form of an enormous black cobra, she was imprisoned long ago in a strange plane known as Jazirah. Dendar constantly plots her escape, and almost succeeded not long ago.

When Dendar was free, she was a slithery, manipulative being who brought many people under her sway. Those following Dendar were often promised their wildest dreams, promises that Dendar had no intentions of honoring.

Lady of Pain

The enigmatic being known as the Lady of Pain is a deity of neutrality, one with the power to rewrite the multiverse, though few know of her true godly nature. The Lady rules Sigil, the city of doors, and violently eschews all worship. She is rarely seen and only ever acts in times of extreme emergency. At all other times, she simply keeps watch over her city and holds the multiverse in check.

Mystryl

Mystryl, the ancient goddess of magic, was the very architect of the Spellweave that all other magic in the multiverse calls upon. In some ways, Mystryl is the Spellweave herself. The wife of Primus, she long ago entered Limbo to investigate a strange anomaly, and never returned.

Mystryl often creates lesser versions of herself, often named Mystra, to police the magic users of most spheres. Though Mystryl gains the memories of these lesser deities, they are independent beings with their own thoughts, emotions, and goals.

[null]

Little is known about the god of chaos [null]. It is known that this god is the counterpart to Primus' law, but the god itself has not been seen nor heard of as far as anyone can remember. It is thought that [null] is in some way connected to Limbo, plane of chaos, but investigations have all turned up dry.

Pandorym

Pandorym was once a nearly unstoppable force of the Astral Void, a sort of living, evil sphere of annihilation the size of a small asteroid. The only way it could be mitigated was to separate its essence from its body, trapping the essence within a crystal sphere. Which crystal sphere and where within it has been lost to history.

Praeteria

Praeteria was the creator of the original humanoid races. Few of her original creations remain, but her legacy can be seen in all life. She rarely appears, usually prefering to exist as a sort of live-giving mana that permeates the multiverse, but when she does appear it is as a dryad.

Primus

Primus is the god of order and law, and the creator of the modron race. In his Hall of Concordance on the plane of Mechanus, he serves to keep the multiverse stable. The modron race is effectively an extension of himself, and it is countless in number.

Quilrith

Quilrith is usually regarded as the leader of the Small Council by those few who know his true nature. He normally takes the form of a dragonborn and travels the multiverse. However, if any threats to the multiverse present themselves, he takes upon the form of a great beast to restore balance.

Quilrith is known for occasionally bringing together groups of adventurers to defend the multiverse. These groups often pull beings from across spheres that never interact with one another, even bringing together the most magical and most technological beings.
