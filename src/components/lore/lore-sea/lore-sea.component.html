Astral Sea

The Rainbow Ocean. The Spellwaves. Wildspace. The Astral Sea is known by many names, and traveled by many peoples. This ocean of iridescent phlogiston is accessed through use of the magical ships called spelljammers, and sailed upon by their crews. Many beings call the Astral Sea home and even more travel there temporarily.

Areas of the Astral Sea

The Astral Sea is composed of three primary areas: the Crystal Fields, Astral Wastes, and Astral Void.

Crystal Fields

Taking up roughly 85% of the known Astral Sea, the Crystal Fields hold countless spheres made of shimmering white crystal. Nothing short of a black hole can so much as scratch these spheres, and entering the various portals that appear on their surfaces sends you to the universe within. Though not an exact replica, the size of a crystal sphere roughly correlates with the size of the universe inside.

Among all crystal spheres, the largest is a massive sphere located at roughly the center of the Astral Sea, Primespace. Thought to have been the first crystal sphere ever, Primespace is among the few crystal spheres that actually spawns more spheres over time. The universe it contains is larger than any other, and contains countless storytellers whose stories are thought to be the basis for many crystal spheres.

Astral Wastes

The Astral Wastes are a sort of shell outside of the Crystal Fields, preventing passage into the Astral Void beyond. Filled with slipstorms, debris from crashed spelljammers or destroyed crystal spheres, dead gods, and evil nega-deities, no being ventures into the Wastes. Many of the threats here won't even grant you the mercy of a clean death, and you will be wishing to die long before you finally give out.

Though the Astral Wastes are all but impassible, there are theories that paths exist through the Wastes into the Astral Void. Magic has determined the existence of the Void, but no paths have been located yet.

Astral Void

Beyond the Astral Wastes lies the mysterious Astral Void. No one knows what awaits in the Void, all that is known about it is that the creators of the multiverse wanted it to be inaccessible and that it holds a magic so ancient that Weavemagic fails to even interact with it. Wild theories abound as to what's here, including cosmic threats, other multiverses, or perhaps even the gods themselves.

Astral Systems

Though most inhabitable locations within the Astral Sea are within crystal spheres, there also exist what are known as Astral Systems, simply solar systems that exist directly in the Astral Sea. Sometimes these systems were once in crystal spheres that were destroyed, but some are native to the Sea.

Phlogiston

The "stuff" of the Astral Sea is an iridescent material called phlogiston (pronounced flow-jis-ton). Not quite a liquid and not quite a gas, phlogiston forms the quite literal waves that spelljammers ride upon. Phlogiston is everywhere in the Astral Sea, and is extremely volatile and flammable. Spelljammers have to be careful not to ignite the phlogiston, lest they be consumed by flames.

The Astral Sea is the only known location where phlogiston can exist. Attempts to transport it into a crystal sphere or to recreate it have all failed, the phlogiston simply disippating when it's ever attempted.

Slipstorms

Sometimes, the phlogiston in the Astral Sea is whipped into a frenzy by unknown means. When this happens enough, it is known as a slipstorm. These resemble storms at sea, but are far more dangerous. The phlogiston in a slipstorm is extra volatile from being stirred, and could explode at any moment. Any spelljammer captain who values their lives usually steers clear of slipstorms by a wide margin. However, some expert spelljammers can actually ride the waves of the slipstorm, using the extra energy to their advantage to boost their speed, and clear the storm before it kills them.

Denizens of the Astral Sea

Factions

Many tiny factions exist within the Astral Sea, but only a few hold any actual sway. These factions can be deadly, but can also be helpful under the right circumstances.

Ioran Ascendancy

The Ioran Ascendancy is an astral empire of sorts governed by praedarens, shark folk who pride themselves in naval domination. Once a reasonable and honorable people, the current Ioran Grand Admiral, leader of their empire, is a corrupt and evil praedaren named Thaddeus Dunwall. Admiral Dunwall has massively expanded Ioran influence in recent years, and his own daughter Erin has started a rebellion against him.

Praetors

The Praetors are an Ioran-adjacent faction of pirates and rebels led by Erin Dunwall, only daughter of the Ioran Grand Admiral. She has been long since tired of her father's machinations, and started the Praetors as a way to one day topple his regime.

Sinclair Corporation

The Sinclair Corporation is a giff-run conglomerate that holds sway over some spheres. A greedy, distrustworthy, and overall shady company, they bully smaller merchant factions into joining them or being destroyed. Since the mysterious death of their CEO, Lucian Sinclair, they have lost some of their power, but remain a dangerous malefactor exploiting anyone they can.

Void People

The Void People are a mysterious and likely entirely fictitious group of beings. Stories about the Void People are common in meeting houses across the Astral Sea's many waystations. No wizard or scholar has managed to prove the existence of the Void People, but any drunk spelljammer topman will be happy to tell you endless stories about how their dentist's third cousin's brother's wife's step-niece's great aunt was abducted by Void People and taken beyond the multiverse.

Native Species

Most beings encountered on the Astral Sea are spelljammers, those traveling from one place to another. However, some species are actually native to the Sea, and almost never enter crystal spheres.

Aartuk

Aartuk are strange plantfolk who sail on living spelljammers. They view war as a form of art, and are fierce zealots who fight for any god of war that will accept them. They are not completely unreasonable though, and can be bartered with under the right circumstances, usually trading information for gemstones, which they are quite fond of.

Astral Dragons

Though many dragons can use magic to enter the Astral Sea themselves, a couple of species are actually native to the Sea: Solar and Lunar dragons.

Solar dragons make their homes in stars, and are generally benevolent creatures. More serpentine than most dragons, their breath weapons are blasts of stellar energy. Their scales start at a fiery red and lighten as they age, eventually becoming a brilliant white.

Lunar dragons are usually evil dragons, and vary between chaos and law. Their color shifts with their personality, darker in the chaotic times and brighter in the lawful times. Their breath weapons are blasts of vacuum that leech the heat away from those they subject to it.

Astral Dreadnoughts

Though usually found in the Astral Fog, dreadnoughts occasionally migrate into the Astral Sea. These hulking monstrosities resemble a cross between a crab and a snake, with a tail that seems to go on forever. Their mere existence bends space around them.

Githyanki

Though technically not native to the Astral Sea as they did not originate there, most githyanki live out their entire lives without ever entrering a crystal sphere. These green-skinned beings were once slaves to illithids, and now most serve the lich queen Vlaakith as little more than pirates. Choosing to eschew spelljammers, most githyanki ride on the backs of red dragons.

Jammer Leeches

Jammer leeches are astral parasites that act as the spelljammer's analouge to barnacles. These small, worm-like creatures attach themselves to the hulls of spelljammers and feed on the magic of the ship. They are exceptionally annoying to remove, as they can camouflage their bodies to resemble the hull they are bound to.

Kindori

Kindori are massive, peaceful, whale-like creatures that travel the Astral Sea in small groups. Also called purrgil or Orcus galacticus, they are capable of natural spelljamming. They come in myriad shapes and subspecies, and some believe that the first spelljammers were inspired by their sightings of these majestic creatures. In fact, kindori skeletons can be used as simple spelljammers themselves.

Psurlons

Psurlons are malevolent, wormlike creatures that were ejected from the Far Realm long ago. They now live on the Astral Sea. They have legs that end in hooves and arms that end in hands with three long fingers. A psurlon's eyeless head resembles that of an earthworm, capped by a maw ringed with teeth. Psurlons adorn themselves in richly colored robes and rarely wear armor or carry weapons.

Scavvers

Scavvers are beasts similar to sharks that roam the Astral Sea as scavengers in pack-like groups called shivers. They are among the few beings that can breathe in the Astral Sea without magical assistance, and often trail behind other large objects, drifting in their trails and gathering whatever they can find. Small scavvers are little more than a nuisance, but large scavvers and especially void scavvers can be extremely dangerous and aggressive. Scavver meat is known to be quite delicious.

Notable Astral Locations

Siren's Rest

Siren's Rest is a space station in a hollowed-out asteroid near Realmspace. It was established by and is the home of the LD pirate clan, a group of fifteen robotic pirate captains from an empire which no longer exists and their crews. Siren's Rest is relatively hidden in an asteroid field, and is fiercely defended by its inhabitants. Of all of the LDs, Scervo (officially known as LD-40616) is the most likely to be encountered elsewhere in the Astral Sea, as he and his crew enjoy adventuring more than the rest. He is also generally considered the unofficial leader of the LD clan, though there is no true leader of the clan and each captain has equal sway.
